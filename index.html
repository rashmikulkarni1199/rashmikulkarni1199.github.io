Hi, welcome to my personal website! I am a Research Assistant with Prof. Suvarna Alladi at the National Institute of Mental Health and Neurosciences (NIMHANS), Bangalore, India.

My current research focuses on the genetic basis of neurodegenerative diseases, with an emphasis on the interpretation of whole-exome sequencing data from Indian patient cohorts. I am particularly interested in variants in genes such as PRNP and MAPT, and in how clinical genomics can improve our understanding of dementia, prion disease and related neurodegenerative disorders.

More broadly, my research interests lie at the interface of human genetics, molecular biology and cellular disease modelling. My previous work has involved CRISPR-based genome editing, splice-switching RNA tools and human cell models of juvenile-onset ALS, and I am interested in using genetically defined models to understand how disease-associated variants lead to cellular dysfunction.

I am especially interested in neurodegeneration genetics, functional disease modelling, lysosomal biology and neuron–glia interactions in disorders such as Parkinson’s disease, ALS and frontotemporal dementia.
