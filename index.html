Hi, welcome to my personal website! I'm a Research Assistant with Prof. Suvarna Alladi at the National Institute of Mental Health and Neuro Sciences (NIMHANS), Bengaluru, India.

My current research focuses on interpreting whole-exome sequencing data from Indian patient cohorts. I'm particularly interested in variants in genes such as PRNP and MAPT, and in how clinical genomics can deepen our understanding of dementia, prion disease, and related neurodegenerative disorders. Although my current work is primarily in clinical bioinformatics, I've spent plenty of time at the bench too!

As a Research Assistant at Queen Mary University of London, I worked with histone modification techniques such as CUT&Tag and CUT&RUN, alongside confocal microscopy. During my master's, I developed CRISPR-Cas9 systems for cleaner knockouts and excisable fluorescent reporter cassettes. My favourite techniques so far are tissue culture and microscopy.

Outside the lab, I enjoy watching movies, reading, and cooking, which to me feels a lot like working in the lab!
